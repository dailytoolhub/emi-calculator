Two-Wheeler Loan EMI Calculation Guide (2026)

A two-wheeler loan allows you to purchase a motorcycle, scooter, or superbike by paying a small initial down payment and financing the remaining amount through manageable Equated Monthly Installments (EMIs).

Why are Bike Loan Interest Rates Higher?

You may notice that two-wheeler loans often have higher interest rates (typically 10.5% to 18%) compared to car or home loans. This is because two-wheelers are subject to much faster depreciation and carry a higher risk profile for lenders. To get the best rate, maintain a CIBIL score above 750 and apply during festive periods when banks offer "Zero Processing Fee" schemes.

Down Payment & LTV Ratio

Most banks and NBFCs offer a Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio of 85% to 95% on the On-Road Price of the bike. This means if you are buying a bike worth ₹1,000,000, you will need to pay roughly ₹10,000 to ₹15,000 upfront as a down payment.

The Hypothecation Process

When you take a bike loan, your RC (Registration Certificate) book will have a 'Hypothecation' stamp indicating that the bank technically owns the vehicle. Once your final EMI is paid, you must obtain an NOC (No Objection Certificate) from your bank and submit it to your local RTO to remove the hypothecation and gain full legal ownership of your vehicle.
