Understanding Personal Loan EMIs (2026)

A personal loan is an unsecured loan, meaning you do not need to provide collateral (like a house or car) to the bank. Because the risk to the bank is higher, personal loans command higher interest rates, typically ranging from 10.50% to 24.00% depending on your CIBIL score.

The Impact of Processing Fees

When calculating your personal loan, remember that banks charge a processing fee (usually 1% to 2.5% of the loan amount). This fee is usually deducted directly from your disbursed amount. So if you need exactly ₹3 Lakhs for a medical emergency, you should apply for ₹3.05 Lakhs to cover the fee.

Pre-Closure Charges

Unlike Home Loans (which have zero pre-closure charges per RBI rules), personal loans usually carry a hefty pre-closure or foreclosure penalty of 2% to 4%. Furthermore, most banks have a "lock-in" period of 6 to 12 months before they allow you to pay off the loan entirely. If you plan to pay off the loan early, factor this penalty into your financial calculations.

Why Tenure Matters

While stretching a personal loan to 5 years lowers your monthly EMI, it drastically increases the total interest you pay to the bank. It is highly recommended to keep personal loan tenures as short as possible (1 to 3 years) to avoid a debt trap.
