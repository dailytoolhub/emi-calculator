Home Loan EMI & Tax Benefits in India (2026)

A home loan is usually the largest financial commitment you will make. Because home loan amounts are large (often ₹50 Lakhs to ₹1 Crore+) and tenures are long (up to 30 years), even a 0.5% difference in your interest rate can save you lakhs of rupees in interest.

Tax Deductions on Home Loans

Under the Indian Income Tax Act, taking a home loan offers massive tax savings:

  • Section 80C: You can claim a deduction of up to ₹1.5 Lakhs per financial year on the Principal repayment portion of your EMI.
  • Section 24(b): You can claim a deduction of up to ₹2 Lakhs per financial year on the Interest repayment portion if the property is self-occupied.

Should You Prepays Your Home Loan?

In the first 5 to 10 years of a 20-year home loan, almost 70% of your EMI goes strictly towards paying the bank's interest, while your actual loan principal barely reduces. Making a small prepayment of just 1 extra EMI per year can reduce your overall 20-year loan tenure by almost 4 years!
