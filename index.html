How EMI is Calculated (2026 Guide)

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is the fixed monthly payment you make to a bank or NBFC to repay your loan. It consists of two parts: the principal repayment and the interest payment.

The Mathematical EMI Formula

E = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

Frequently Asked Questions

How does making a prepayment affect my EMI?

Making a part-prepayment reduces your outstanding principal amount. You can choose to reduce your EMI or decrease your loan tenure.

Does a higher CIBIL score reduce my EMI?

Yes. In India, a CIBIL score of 750 or above usually qualifies you for concessional interest rates, which lowers your EMI.
